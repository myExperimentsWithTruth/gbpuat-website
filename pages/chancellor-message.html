A message from the Chancellor

Lt. Gen. (Retd.) Gurmit Singh

Chancellor of Govind Ballabh Pant University of Agriculture & Technology · Governor of Uttarakhand.

The Govind Ballabh Pant University of Agriculture & Technology occupies a distinctive place in the history of Indian higher education: it was the first agricultural university to be established in the country, and it carries the founding vision of a young republic committed to rural transformation.

On 17 November 1960, at a moment when the newly independent nation was building the institutional foundations of its food security, Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru dedicated this university to the service of the country. In the six and a half decades since, Pantnagar has trained scientists, administrators and citizens who have shaped Indian agriculture and its allied sciences.

As Chancellor, I convey my warmest wishes to the students, faculty, staff, alumni and stakeholders of this institution. The university’s work — in released crop varieties, in research and extension, in the training of the next generation — matters not only to Uttarakhand, but to Indian agriculture as a whole.

A university with a public mandate

Pantnagar was conceived on the land-grant model, in partnership with the University of Illinois. Its purpose has always been public: research that reaches farms, teaching that meets the field, and extension that treats the rural sector as an equal partner. This mandate remains as relevant now as it was in 1960.

A tradition of contribution

The university has released two hundred and seventy-six crop and plant varieties for Indian farmers, produced generations of researchers whose work is recognised nationally and internationally, and remained active through its Kisan Mela, its Krishi Vigyan Kendra, and its extension network. These are not achievements to be listed for their own sake; they are the outward expression of a university that continues to earn its founding purpose.

The measure of an agricultural university is not what it publishes, but what it releases — a variety, a technology, a practice, a graduate. On this measure, Pantnagar continues to give account of itself.

To the students of Pantnagar

The opportunity you have here — to study on sixteen thousand acres of a working, teaching, researching campus — is one of the finest that Indian higher education offers. I ask you to use it fully, and to carry the discipline of this place into whatever role you take up afterwards.

— Lt. Gen. (Retd.) Gurmit Singh
Chancellor, GBPUA&T · Governor of Uttarakhand
