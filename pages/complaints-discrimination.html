Form · Risk

This page originally contains a submission form. A discrimination complaint page collects sensitive personal information from complainants and often names other individuals. The submission surface is not implemented in this demonstration; complaints continue through the Registrar's office and the UGC portal. Migration must be reviewed for personal data handling and integration with the existing university systems.

The university operates under the University Grants Commission's Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal of Discrimination against Students Regulations, 2012. Complaints of caste-based, religious or other discrimination are treated as statutory matters.

Who can file

Any student, faculty member or staff of the university, or their nominated representative, can file a complaint.

How to file

  1. Write a signed complaint to the Registrar's office, or through the SC/ST Cell for matters covered by that cell.
  2. Complaints can also be filed directly with the UGC through its central complaint portal.
  3. Anonymous complaints are considered on merit but the response is more limited.

Response

Complaints are reviewed by the statutory committee, which is bound by the UGC Regulations. Findings and actions are communicated to the complainant in confidence.
