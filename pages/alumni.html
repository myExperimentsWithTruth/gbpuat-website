Sixty-five years of graduates

Alumni of Pantnagar

The largest continuing community this institution has — and, in scale and reach, one of the largest alumni networks any Indian public university possesses.

The alumni of Govind Ballabh Pant University of Agriculture & Technology are today at work across Indian and international agriculture, veterinary science, research, government, industry, banking, food and dairy, and public life. Sixty-five graduating batches, spread widely, form a public asset that few Indian institutions can match at scale.

The Alumni Alma Mater Advancement Association (4A)

The formal alumni association of the university is the Alumni Alma Mater Advancement Association, commonly known as 4A. It runs the alumni portal at pantnagar4a.in. The Vice-Chancellor is the ex officio President of the Association.

What 4A does

  • Maintains the alumni directory and enables reconnection between batchmates and across cohorts.
  • Runs city-chapter events and international-chapter gatherings.
  • Co-ordinates the annual Alumni Meet on campus.
  • Facilitates alumni-instituted scholarships, chairs and infrastructure gifts to the university.
  • Publishes the alumni newsletter and news of notable alumni achievements.

Notable alumni

The alumni network of the university includes senior figures in Indian and international public service, science and industry. Two who are named on university communications and easily verifiable:

  • Sri Vinay Mohan Kwatra — Foreign Secretary of India (referenced on the College of Agriculture page as a graduate of that College).
  • Dr. Islam A. Siddiqui — Chief Agricultural Negotiator in the office of the United States Trade Representative under President Barack Obama, with the rank of Ambassador (also a graduate of the College of Agriculture).
