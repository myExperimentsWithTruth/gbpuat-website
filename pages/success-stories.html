The story of an agricultural university is told less in prospectuses than in the outcomes it has produced — in the varieties grown by farmers, the tools they use, the graduates who move Indian agriculture forward, and the practices that outlast individual careers.

276 released crop and plant varieties

Since 1960, Pantnagar has released two hundred and seventy-six crop and plant varieties for Indian farmers — the most consequential number in the university's history. These span rice, wheat, sugarcane, pulses, oilseeds, vegetables and fruits.

Padma Shri — Dr. Prem Lal Gautam

A former Vice-Chancellor of the university was conferred the Padma Shri in 2026 for sustained contribution to Indian agricultural science.

IAUA Best PhD Thesis

A Pantnagar doctoral student took the Indian Agricultural Universities Association's Best PhD Thesis Award in 2026.

ICAR Best Centre Award

The AICRP Forage Research Programme at Pantnagar was recognised by ICAR as its Best Centre for the year.

The extension record

Pantnagar's extension work — through KVKs, SAMETI, ATIC, the Training and Visit unit, and the annual Kisan Mela — has been recognised as a national reference model for agricultural extension.
