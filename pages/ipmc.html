The Intellectual Property Management Cell (IPMC) is the university's dedicated unit for identifying, protecting, licensing and commercialising research-derived intellectual property.

What IPMC does

  • Invention disclosure, evaluation and patent filing
  • Trademark and copyright registration
  • Licensing to industry and technology transfer
  • Awareness workshops for faculty and students
  • Support to the Directorate of Experiment Station

Patents portfolio

The university holds patents across agricultural implements, biological products, diagnostic tools and food-processing technologies — a growing portfolio maintained by IPMC.
