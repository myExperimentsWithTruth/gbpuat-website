About the Directorate

The Directorate of Extension Education carries the university's research into the villages, blocks and districts around Pantnagar — and returns questions from the field back to the laboratory.

Extension is not a downstream activity at a land-grant university; it is a first-order function. Every crop variety, technology, animal-husbandry practice or post-harvest method released by the university reaches its intended user through extension.

Structure

The Directorate is organised around specialised units:

  • Agricultural Technology Information Centre (ATIC)
  • Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs)
  • State Agricultural Management and Extension Training Institute (SAMETI)
  • Training and Visit Unit
  • Crop Advisory

Agricultural Technology Information Centre (ATIC)

ATIC is the single-window interface between the university and the farmer. It provides advisory services, distributes tested seeds and planting material, and disseminates university publications.

Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK)

The Krishi Vigyan Kendras are frontline extension centres that operate in specific districts and take university technologies to the farm gate through demonstrations, training and support.

SAMETI

The State Agricultural Management and Extension Training Institute delivers training to state extension officers, KVK staff and progressive farmers.

Training and Visit Unit

The Training and Visit Unit organises structured visits by extension personnel to farmer groups on a regular cycle, so that field observations and university recommendations are exchanged continuously.

Crop Advisory

Crop Advisory issues time-sensitive recommendations to farmers based on weather patterns, pest and disease outbreaks, and agronomic conditions. It is the practical face of the university's research programme for the current season.
