Zero tolerance

Anti-Ragging at Pantnagar

Ragging in any form is a criminal offence and is prohibited on this campus. The university operates a statutory Anti-Ragging Committee and squad, and a 24×7 helpline.

Ragging is prohibited by the University Grants Commission and by the Uttarakhand Prohibition of Ragging Act. Every student admitted to the university, and every parent or guardian, signs an affidavit accepting this. The university enforces the prohibition through a statutory committee, an active squad, and a helpline available around the clock.

What ragging means

Ragging includes any act, spoken or written, that has the effect of teasing, treating with rudeness, causing physical or psychological harm, or asking a student to do something that raises apprehension, shame or embarrassment. It includes financial extortion and any misuse of power over a junior student.

Who to contact, immediately

  • Anti-Ragging Helpline (24×7) — 1800-180-5522 (UGC national helpline)
  • Anti-Ragging Committee, Pantnagar — contact the Registrar’s office
  • Anti-Ragging Squad — a rotating student and faculty patrol

How to file a complaint

Any student who has experienced or witnessed ragging can:

  1. Call the 24×7 national helpline immediately.
  2. File an online complaint at antiragging.in, which is monitored by the UGC.
  3. Approach any member of the university’s Anti-Ragging Committee or Squad in person.
  4. Write to the Vice-Chancellor’s office. Complaints are treated as confidential.
Complaints are acted upon quickly. Retaliation against a complainant is treated as a separate and more serious offence.

Consequences

Ragging can result in cancellation of admission, suspension from the university, debarment from examinations, expulsion from hostel, rustication for up to two semesters, and permanent expulsion. It can also result in criminal proceedings under the state Act and the Indian Penal Code.
