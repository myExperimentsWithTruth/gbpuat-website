About the Directorate

The Directorate of Experiment Station co-ordinates the university’s research programme across its constituent colleges, research centres and outstations. Its founding remit is directly connected to the university’s founding purpose: research that reaches the farm.

The Directorate is responsible for the planning, execution and review of research projects, for the maintenance of the university’s field and laboratory infrastructure, and for the release of technologies and varieties to the public system. It works closely with the Directorate of Extension Education so that research outputs travel to farmers, and with ICAR institutes so that university work is aligned with national priorities.

Research Centres

Research at Pantnagar is organised through a network of centres and outstations spanning the different agro-climatic zones of the state and beyond. Each centre carries specialised mandates — from crop improvement to fisheries, from soil science to post-harvest technology.

  • Crop Research Centre, Pantnagar
  • Vegetable Research Centre
  • Norman E. Borlaug Crop Research Centre
  • Hill Campus, Ranichauri
  • Fisheries Research Centre
  • Livestock Research Centre
  • Sugarcane Research Centre, Kashipur

Research Highlights

Since 1960, university researchers have produced work of national and international significance across agriculture, veterinary science, community science, fisheries and applied engineering. Highlights of the current period include contribution to the National Agricultural Higher Education Project, ongoing work on climate-resilient varieties, and multi-institutional projects on digital agriculture and AI.

Two hundred and seventy-six crop and plant varieties, released for Indian farmers since 1960 — a measure of what an agricultural university is for.

New Technologies

The Directorate maintains a rolling portfolio of technologies developed at the university — agronomic, mechanical, biological and digital. These technologies are transferred to farmers through the Extension Education directorate, to industry through licensing, and to policy through the university’s formal advisory role in state and central bodies.

Patents

The university has been granted a substantial portfolio of patents over the years, across agricultural implements, food processing, biological products and diagnostic tools. The Intellectual Property Management Cell (IPMC) supports researchers through disclosure, filing and licensing.

Released Varieties

The clearest measure of the Directorate’s work is the varieties it releases. Two hundred and seventy-six crop and plant varieties have been released since 1960, spanning rice, wheat, sugarcane, pulses, oilseeds, vegetables and fruits. Many of these are grown widely across northern India and remain part of the national release catalogue.
