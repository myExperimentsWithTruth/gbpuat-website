Central Library

Print and digital collections for the eight colleges and the Directorate research programme. ONOS and INFLIBNET access for every student and faculty member on campus.

The Central Library is the working reading and research space of the university. It maintains the print, digital and archival collections that support teaching across the eight colleges and research across the Directorate of Experiment Station.

Collections

The Library holds textbooks, monographs, reference works and periodicals across agriculture, veterinary science, community science, fisheries, engineering, humanities and management. The current periodicals section supports faculty in the disciplines where new work is published continuously; the archival section preserves older editions relevant to the university’s long-form crop and animal research.

Digital access

All students and faculty on campus have access to the following:

  • ONOS — One Nation One Subscription, giving campus access to publisher collections centrally.
  • INFLIBNET / N-LIST — UGC-funded e-resources and databases.
  • Subject databases in agriculture, veterinary and food science, negotiated by the university directly.

Hours and services

The Library is open through the working week, with extended hours during examination periods and on request. Services include reference support, inter-library loan, thesis submission and archival access.

Library dues

Students are asked to clear all outstanding Library dues before the annual deadline — this is required for semester registration and for the release of final results. Notices are issued periodically through the year.
