A message from

Prof. Shivendra Kumar Kashyap

29th Vice-Chancellor of Govind Ballabh Pant University of Agriculture & Technology — assumed charge April 2026.

On 17 November 1960 Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru dedicated to the nation India’s first agricultural university on the fields of Pantnagar. Sixty-five years on, this institution’s founding purpose — the systematic application of science and technology to the rural sector — is more urgent, not less.

Pantnagar was conceived on the land-grant model, in partnership with the University of Illinois, at a time when the country needed both food security and rural livelihoods at scale. In the decades since, the university has released two hundred and seventy-six crop and plant varieties for Indian farmers, produced generations of scientists, administrators and entrepreneurs, and remained a working farm at every layer of its academic life.

As I assume charge as the twenty-ninth Vice-Chancellor, my commitment to this institution is threefold.

Teaching that meets the field

Our eight colleges — Agriculture, Veterinary and Animal Sciences, Technology, Basic Sciences and Humanities, Community Science, Fisheries, Agribusiness Management, and Post Graduate Studies — must continue to graduate students who can go straight from the lecture theatre to the field, laboratory or firm. That link between classroom and application is the university’s founding claim; we will strengthen it.

Research that reaches the farm

The Directorate of Experiment Station co-ordinates a network of field stations, and its output is measured in released varieties, patents and demonstrable field practice — not in publications alone. In the coming period, digital agriculture and artificial-intelligence-driven research will be central to how we frame this work, without displacing the long-form crop, animal and technology research that Pantnagar is known for.

Pantnagar’s work leaves the laboratory. That is the discipline of a land-grant university, and the reason the institution matters beyond its campus.

Extension that treats farmers as partners

Through the Directorate of Extension Education, KVK, SAMETI and ATIC, the university remains embedded in the villages, blocks and districts around us. The annual Kisan Mela — in its 119th edition in March 2026 — is the most public expression of this relationship, but the day-to-day work of extension is the more consequential part.

Alumni, the largest community we have

Sixty-five years of graduates form the widest constituency this institution possesses. Their capability, deployed at scale, is what will let a public university build tools it would otherwise have to buy, and reach places it would otherwise be closed to. In this term we will make the connection to alumni structural, not sentimental.

I invite students, faculty, alumni and every well-wisher of this institution to write to me. Pantnagar has always been at its strongest when the community around it was closest.

— Prof. Shivendra Kumar Kashyap
Vice-Chancellor
