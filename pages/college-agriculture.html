College I

College of Agriculture

The founding college of the university — and the reason it exists. Agronomy, plant breeding, soil science, entomology, plant pathology, horticulture, and the disciplines that grew out of them.

When Pantnagar was dedicated in 1960, its purpose was to bring science and technology to the rural sector at scale. That purpose is the College of Agriculture’s founding purpose. The College remains the largest of the eight, the oldest, and the one closest to the university’s public mandate.

Programmes

The College offers programmes at every level of the university:

  • B.Sc. (Hons.) Agriculture — the flagship four-year undergraduate degree, admission via the Entrance Examination.
  • M.Sc. Agriculture — across ten disciplines, admission via the Entrance Examination or ICAR AIEEA.
  • Ph.D. — doctoral research in every department of the College.

Research and released varieties

The College is central to the university’s two-hundred-and-seventy-six released varieties. Rice, wheat, sugarcane, pulses, oilseeds, vegetables and fruits released from Pantnagar carry the imprint of the College’s crop-breeding, agronomy and horticulture work — work that continues today under the direction of the Directorate of Experiment Station.

The College of Agriculture is where the founding claim of the university is redeemed — research that reaches the farm, and teaching that meets the field.

Kisan Mela and public engagement

Every March the College is at the centre of the annual Pantnagar Kisan Mela, in its 119th edition in 2026. The Mela is the university’s most public rite — four days of demonstrations, exhibitions, and direct engagement with farmers from Uttarakhand and neighbouring states.

Faculty and infrastructure

The College maintains its own experiment plots, laboratories, greenhouses, seed processing facilities, and specialised units for entomology, plant pathology and post-harvest work. The Norman E. Borlaug Crop Research Centre, on the main campus, is one of the university’s signature research locations.
