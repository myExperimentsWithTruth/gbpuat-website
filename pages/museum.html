The Pantnagar Museum brings together the artefacts, documents, photographs and instruments that tell the story of India's first agricultural university. It is a resource for students, faculty, visitors and alumni.

What is on display

  • Founding-era photographs and correspondence, including the dedication of the university by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru in 1960.
  • Instruments and equipment used by the founding faculty across the crop, animal, and technology sciences.
  • Released crop varieties and their historical significance, arranged by decade.
  • Records of the annual Kisan Mela — posters, photographs and reports from every edition.
  • Alumni contributions to Indian agriculture, research and public life.

Visiting the Museum

The Museum is open through the working week. Guided tours are arranged on request for school and college groups, delegations, and visiting scholars.

Contribute

Alumni, families of former faculty, and other stakeholders are invited to contribute artefacts, photographs and personal papers relating to the university's history. The Museum accepts material as donation or on loan.
