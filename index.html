A note on the founding

After Independence, development of the rural sector was among the primary concerns of the Government of India. On 17 November 1960, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru dedicated to the nation India’s first agricultural university — conceived on the land-grant model in partnership with the University of Illinois, and named after Pandit Govind Ballabh Pant.

The seed was land, science and the rural sector. Sixty-five years on, the harvest is measured in released varieties, patents, alumni and public trust.

Colleges

Eight colleges, one contiguous campus.

The university is organised into eight colleges, each with its own faculty, laboratories, farms or field stations, and admission stream — all sharing sixteen thousand acres and a single institutional purpose.

  1. 01AgricultureAgronomy, plant breeding, soil science, entomology, plant pathology, horticulture — the founding college.
  2. 02Veterinary & Animal SciencesClinical veterinary practice, animal husbandry, dairy science, public health — teaching hospital on campus.
  3. 03TechnologyEngineering and technology for agriculture, food processing, computer science and applied streams.
  4. 04Basic Sciences & HumanitiesFoundational sciences, mathematics, statistics, languages and social sciences.
  5. 05Community ScienceNutrition, human development, resource management, textiles and community outreach.
  6. 06FisheriesAquaculture, fisheries resource management, aquatic biology and post-harvest technology.
  7. 07Agribusiness ManagementManagement for agricultural value chains, agri-input industry and rural enterprise.
  8. 08Post Graduate StudiesThe umbrella for master’s and doctoral programmes across the university.
