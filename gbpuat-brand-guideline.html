Overview

This document captures and rationalises the visual identity used across the university's public website into a usable brand reference for consistent internal application.

Status. This is an unofficial guideline extracted from the live website. It is not an authorised university brand manual. Colours and type were sampled from gbpuat.ac.in on 04 July 2026. The crest is the university's official mark, embedded directly in this file so it renders in any viewer, online or offline. Where the site used framework defaults or inconsistent values, they have been rationalised into a coherent system and noted in the Source Notes.

The identity reflects the institution's character: a land-grant agricultural university with a heritage rooted in earth tones, balanced by clear neutral typography for a bilingual (Hindi and English) audience. The palette centres on institutional maroon and earthen rust, supported by an agricultural green and a fresh teal accent.

Logo

The university crest is the primary identity mark. It carries the full institutional name in Hindi and English and should always be reproduced from the high-resolution master.

On light backgrounds
On dark / maroon — place on a white keyline or panel

Clear space & minimum size

Maintain clear space around the crest equal to at least 25% of its width on all sides. Do not reproduce the crest below 40px (screen) or 15mm (print) in diameter, below which the inner detail and text become illegible.

Do

  • Use the official high-resolution crest master.
  • Preserve the circular proportion and full detail.
  • Place on white or a white panel when over colour or photography.
  • Keep generous clear space around the mark.

Don't

  • Recolour, flatten or add effects to the crest.
  • Stretch, rotate or crop the mark.
  • Place the crest on a busy background without a panel.
  • Recreate or approximate the emblem from memory.

Typography

The site relies on Arial for interface and body text with Times New Roman for formal and titular use. A dedicated Devanagari face is recommended to give the Hindi identity equal footing.

Primary — Arial / Helvetica

Aa Bb Cc

The workhorse for body copy, navigation, tables and all interface text. Universally available; reliable across web and print.

Source Notes

How this reference was derived and what was rationalised.

  • Colour, type and logo sampled from the live site gbpuat.ac.in on 04 July 2026 using computed styles across the homepage.
  • The most frequent deliberate brand colours were maroon (#79211C), rust (#962901), teal (#26CCCA) and green (#008000). These were kept.
  • A prominent blue (#0D6EFD) was identified as a Bootstrap framework default rather than a brand choice and was excluded; the deliberate steel/sky blues were retained for links only.
  • The site has no formal typographic scale; the hierarchy shown here is a recommendation consistent with observed usage (Arial body, Times titles).
  • The crest is the university's official mark from gbpuat.ac.in/img/Logo1.png, downscaled and embedded directly in this file (base64) so it is fully self-contained. For high-resolution print, obtain a vector master (SVG/EPS) from the university.
  • This is an unofficial extraction for reference and consistency, not an authorised brand manual.
